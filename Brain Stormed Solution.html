Let's brainstorm a list of gender problems and issues from a historical perspective and then identify which ones continue to be relevant in both international and Philippine contexts

  • Suffrage and Political Representation:
    Historical Problem: Women's struggle for the right to vote and participate in political processes.
    Relevance: Ongoing concerns about equal political representation and participation globally.

  • Gender-Based Violence:
    Historical Problem: Systemic violence against women, including domestic violence, sexual assault, and harassment.
    Relevance: Continues to be a pervasive issue worldwide, with ongoing efforts to address and prevent gender-based violence.

  • Employment Discrimination:
    Historical Problem: Limited access to certain professions and unequal pay for the same work based on gender.
    Relevance: Unequal pay and limited access to certain professions persist globally, contributing to gender wage gaps.

  • Reproductive Rights:
    Historical Problem: Limited access to reproductive health services and lack of control over reproductive choices.
    Relevance: Ongoing debates and challenges related to reproductive rights globally, including issues of access to contraception and safe abortion.

  • Education Disparities:
    Historical Problem: Limited educational opportunities for women, including barriers to higher education.
    Relevance: Although progress has been made, disparities in educational opportunities and outcomes still exist in various parts of the world.

  • Gender Stereotypes and Social Expectations:
    Historical Problem: Strict societal norms and expectations dictating how men and women should behave.
    Relevance: Persistent gender stereotypes and societal expectations impact individuals' choices and opportunities globally.
