Brainstorming Process for Gender Issues

    In this first page, we will explain and show you the 3 Brainstorming techniques we used to be able to compose and gatter information and Identify Gender Issues that occur
not just in the Philippines but also world wide.

Let's go through all the Brainstorming Techniques!

   First : Mind Mapping
  1. Exploration: Initiated the process by creating a mind map to visually organize key themes related to historical gender issues. Identified categories such as suffrage, violence, discrimination, health, education, and stereotypes.
  2. Connections: Visualized the interconnectedness of these themes, recognizing their historical significance and relevance to contemporary global and Philippine contexts.

   Second: Free Association
  1. Divergent Thinking: Allowed for free association to generate a diverse list of historical gender problems. Jotted down ideas without immediate judgment to ensure inclusivity of various issues.
  2. Expansive Exploration: Encouraged a wide range of considerations, from suffrage movements to reproductive rights, to capture the complexity of gender issues over time.

   Third: Nominal Group Technique
  1. Individual Idea Generation: Considered various historical gender problems, drawing from both global and Philippine contexts. Evaluated the importance of suffrage, violence, discrimination, and health as central themes.
  2. Structured Integration: Organized the generated ideas in a structured and coherent manner, ensuring a logical flow in the response. This technique allowed for a collective integration of knowledge and perspectives.
